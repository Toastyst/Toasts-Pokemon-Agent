### Step 45 (cont.): Viridian Gym — Giovanni Battle

```
Rhyhorn Lv.45 — Stomp, Tail Whip, Fury Attack, Horn Drill
Dugtrio Lv.42 — Growl, Dig, Sand-Attack, Slash
Nidoqueen Lv.44 — Scratch, Tail Whip, Body Slam, Poison Sting
Nidoking Lv.45 — Tackle, Horn Attack, Poison Sting, Thrash
Rhydon Lv.50 — Stomp, Tail Whip, Fissure, Horn Drill
```

**Strategy:**
All Ground/Rock types. **Surf/Bubblebeam is 4× effective** on Rhyhorn and Rhydon!
- Rhydon: 4× weak to Water AND 4× weak to Grass. One Surf will kill it.
- Dugtrio is fast with Slash (high crit rate) — use a Water type to resist.
- Nidoking/Nidoqueen: Ground/Poison. Surf is 4× effective! Also, Psychic is 2× effective (vs. Poison).
- Giovanni's Fissure (from Rhydon) is an OHKO move — pray for a miss or kill Rhydon first.

**Recommended team position:** Lead with Blastoise or Venusaur. Hyper Beam the Dugtrio before it outspeeds.

**Memory check:** D356 AND 0x80 != 0 → EarthBadge obtained.
**Receive TM27 (Fissure) and EarthBadge.**

**Now ALL badges obtained. ALL traded Pokémon obey. HM field moves unlocked.**

### Step 46: Victory Road (Maps — Route 23 entrance)

**SAVE before Victory Road.** Stock up:
- Full Restore × 15+
- Revive × 10+
- Full Heal × 10+
- Elixer × 5+

**Route 23:** After getting 8 badges, the previously blocked guard moves. Enter Victory Road.

**Victory Road is a 3-floor cave:**

**Floor 1 (1F):**
1. Enter the cave.
2. Use Strength to push boulders into holes. Fall through holes to lower floors.
3. Collect Ultra Ball and Full R

estore (hidden items).

**Floor 2 (2F):**
1. Navigate to the north-east section.
2. Push a boulder south into a hole.
3. Cross the ledge and fall through a hole.
4. Navigate to exit (east or west stair).

**Floor 3 (3F):**
1. Multiple boulder puzzles.
2. Push all boulders into the correct holes to create paths.
3. Navigate to the north exit (Indigo Plateau entrance).

**Trainer battles inside Victory Road:**
- Cooltrainer Lass and other high-level trainers (Lv. 40–50).
- One "invisible" trainer (boulder must be on correct hole not to trap you). **SAVE before stepping onto the tile where this trainer appears.**

**Wild Pokémon in Victory Road:**
- Machoke, Graveler, Onix, Marowak (high levels 40–50).

### Step 48: Elite Four — LORELEI (Room 1)

**Team:**
```
Dewgong Lv.54 — Growl, Aurora Beam, Rest, Take Down
Cloyster Lv.53 — Supersonic, Clamp, Aurora Beam, Spike Cannon
Slowbro Lv.54 — Water Gun, Growl, Withdraw, Amnesia
Jynx Lv.56 — Doubleslap, Ice Punch, Body Slam, Thrash
Lapras Lv.56 — Body Slam, Confuse Ray, Hydro Pump, Blizzard
```

**Strategy:**
- **Electric moves (Thunderbolt) are 2× effective on ALL five Pokémon.**
- Recommended: Zapdos (Thunderbolt), Electabuzz, or any Electric TM user.
- Slowbro can use Amnesia (boosting Special dramatically) → kill before 2 uses.
- Dewgong uses Rest (full heal) → finish it before Jynx/Lapras.
- Lapras: Blizzard has high freeze risk (permanent in Gen 1 without Fire move).

**After Lorelei:** Doors open to Bruno's room. YOU HEAL AUTOMATICALLY between each Elite Four member.

### Step 49: Elite Four — BRUNO (Room 2)

**Team:**
```
Onix Lv.53 — Rock Throw, Rage, Slam, Harden
Hitmonchan Lv.55 — Ice Punch, Mega Punch, Thunderpunch, Counter
Hitmonlee Lv.55 — Jump Kick, Focus Energy, Hi Jump Kick, Mega Kick
Onix Lv.56 — Rock Throw, Rage, Slam, Harden
Machamp Lv.58 — Leer, Focus Energy, Fissure, Submission
```

**Strategy:**
- Psychic (Psybeam/Psychic) = 2× vs. Hitmonlee, Hitmonchan, Machamp.
- **Onix: Surf or Vine Whip = 4× effective (Rock+Ground + Water or Grass).**
- Hitmonlee's Jump Kick: Use Fly. While airborne, Jump Kick misses and Hitmonlee takes ~50% max HP crash damage.
- Machamp's Fissure (OHKO) → Speed tie or priority: Quick Attack can work, or use Substitute.

**Recommended:** Strong Psychic type (Alakazam) for Fighting types + Blastoise for Onix.

### Step 50: Elite Four — AGATHA (Room 3)

**Team:**
```
Gengar Lv.56 — Confuse Ray, Night Shade, Hypnosis, Dream Eater
Golbat Lv.56 — Supersonic, Confuse Ray, Wing Attack, Haze
Haunter Lv.55 — Confuse Ray, Night Shade, Hypnosis, Dream Eater
Arbok Lv.58 — Bite, Glare, Screech, Acid
Gengar Lv.60 — Confuse Ray, Night Shade, Toxic, Dream Eater
```

**Strategy:**
- Psychic types are **immune to Ghost moves in Gen 1**. Use Psychic!
- Gengar/Haunter's combo: Hypnosis → Dream Eater. Use switch to wake up.
- **Alakazam with Psychic sweeps this room.** Speed tie is important.
- Golbat: 2× weak to Electric, Ice, Rock.
- Arbok: 2× weak to Psychic, Ground.
- Acid from Arbok can lower Special → Full Restore if Arbok poisons.

### Step 51: Elite Four — LANCE (Room 4)

**Team:**
```
Gyarados Lv.58 — Hydro Pump, Dragon Rage, Leer, Hyper Beam
Dragonair Lv.56 — Agility, Slam, Dragon Rage, Hyper Beam
Dragonair Lv.56 — Agility, Slam, Dragon Rage, Hyper Beam
Aerodactyl Lv.60 — Supersonic, Take Down, Bite, Hyper Beam
Dragonite Lv.62 — Agility, Slam, Barrier, Hyper Beam
```

**Strategy:**
- **Gyarados: THUNDERBOLT = 4× effective (Water + Flying). Instant KO from high-Spec Electric.**
- Dragon types: Ice Beam (4× vs. Dragonite's Dragon/Flying!) or just overpower.
- Dragonair uses Agility multiple times → outspeeds everything after 2+ uses.
- Hyper Beam: If it KOs, no recharge → Lance gets to act next turn. If it doesn't KO, Lance must recharge → free turn for you.

**Recommended:** Zapdos (Thunderbolt for Gyarados, then Ice Beam for Dragons). Or Blastoise with Thunderbolt + Ice Beam.

### Step 52: CHAMPION — BLUE (Room 5)

**WARNING: There IS one heal (Auto-heal after Lance → you enter the Champion room). SAVE BEFORE THE CHAMPION IF POSSIBLE (not practical — just be prepared).**

**Team (player chose Squirtle → rival has Bulbasaur line):**
```
Pidgeot Lv.60 — Wing Attack, Mirror Move, Sky Attack, Whirlwind
Alakazam Lv.57 — Psybeam, Psychic, Reflect, Recover
Rhydon Lv.59 — Leer, Tail Whip, Fury Attack, Horn Drill
Gyrados Lv.59 — Hydro Pump, Dragon Rage, Leer, Hyper Beam
Exeggutor Lv.59 — Stomp, Barrage, Hypnosis
Venusaur Lv.63 — Razor Leaf, Poisonpowder, Mega Drain, Sleep Powder
```

*(Team order and moves vary slightly based on your starter choice.)*

**Battle plan:**
1. **Pidgeot:** Reflect can be annoying. Use Electric moves (Thunderbolt). Psychic works (Flying).
2. **Alakazam:** Fast and strong. Use Night Shade/Horn Drill or strong physical moves. Reflect from Alakazam can block your physical moves → use special.
3. **Rhydon:** Surf = 4× effective. One-shot.
4. **Gyarados:** Thunderbolt = 4× effective. One shot.
5. **Exeggutor:** Psychic = 2× effective. Ice = 2×. Bug = not very effective (Grass/Poison typing). Use Blastoise Ice Beam or Psychic move.
6. **Venusaur:** Solar Beam is powerful but takes 2 turns. Psychic = 2× (effective vs. Poison). Fire, Ice = 2×. Kill before Solar Beam charges.

**⚠️ If a Pokémon faints, Blue's next Pokémon comes out immediately. If YOUR last Pokémon faints, the game ends and you are sent back to the Pokémon Center outside. You must start the ENTIRE Elite Four over.**
